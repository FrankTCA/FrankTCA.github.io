Religions In Africa

  • Christianity

    The religion about God, and Jesus who saved the world.

  • Islam

    The belief in Allah

Description:

Christianity is the religion based on a singular god, who made the world, and that his son, Jesus, saved the world from sin when the people were sinning most.

Sabbath:

Every Sunday, the Christians go to church on the sabbath, and are forbidden from working.

Main Book:

The Bible is the Christians' main book. It describes the history of the world and all of the information needed to be a Christian.

Name of God(s):

In Christianity, there is a trinity which makes up one god. This consists of God the Father (There is no proper name for him), Jesus, or the son of God, and the Holy Spirit, which is a being which possesses other beings in order to help the trinity.

Main Ideals:

The main central ideals of Christianity is pretty much just doing the right thing. If you do something wrong, you can ask for forgiveness later and you will be removed of your sins.

Description:

The Muslim beliefs say that you must spend your whole life to please Allah, so that one may gain eternal salvation.

Sabbath:

Friday is the sabbath for Islam, and they also, similar to Christians, do not work during the sabbath.

Main Book:

The Islamic holy book is the Quaran.

Names of God(s)

There is only one god in the Muslim religion, and it is Allah.

Main Ideals:

The main Muslim ideal is that they must please Allah in order to move on to the afterlife.
